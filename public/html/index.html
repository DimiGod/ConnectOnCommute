Why We Exist

In the US and across the world we are experiencing one of the most detrimental loneliness epidemics in our history. It's no secret that our "Caveman brains" (or limbic system) has evolved a need for social acceptance and interaction- so much so that if loneliness becomes chronic it can be as hard on our bodies as obesity or smoking a pack of ciggarets per day. 53% of Americans believe that noone knows them well, and 47% report always or often feeling lonely.At a time of constant connection to everyone through the internet, social media, and our phones- why is it that so many people feel more disconnected than ever? Loneliness is a shared human experience, so why are we so afraid to talk to each other?
